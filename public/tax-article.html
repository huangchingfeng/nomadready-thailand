Thailand Tax Deadline 2026: What Every Digital Nomad Needs to Know Before April 8

If you spent more than 180 days in Thailand in 2025, you might owe taxes. Here's what you need to know.

The Deadline

  • Paper filing: March 31, 2026
  • E-filing (via rd.go.th): April 8, 2026

Miss it? You'll face a fine of up to 2,000 THB plus a 1.5% monthly surcharge on any unpaid tax.

Who Needs to File?

You're a Thai tax resident if you spent 180 days or more in Thailand during 2025. This applies regardless of your visa type — tourist visa, DTV, or visa-exempt entries all count.

If your annual income exceeds 120,000 THB (single) or 220,000 THB (married), you must file.

The Big Change: Foreign Income Is Now Taxable

Since January 1, 2024, foreign-sourced income remitted to Thailand is subject to Thai taxation. This is the biggest change affecting digital nomads.

What this means: If you earned money from a US/EU client while sitting in a Chiang Mai café, and then transferred that money to your Thai bank account, it's potentially taxable.

Exception: Income earned before January 1, 2024 that you transfer to Thailand is still exempt. Keep documentation proving when the income was earned.

LTR visa holders may be exempt from tax on foreign-source income — check with a tax advisor.

Which Form to Use

  • PND 91: Salary income only
  • PND 90: Multiple income sources (this is what most nomads need — freelance income, rental income, investment returns, etc.)

How to File

  1. Get a Thai Tax ID from your local Revenue Department Office
  2. Gather your income documentation
  3. File online at rd.go.th or in person
  4. Pay any tax owed

Tax Rates

Thailand uses progressive rates:

  • 0–150,000 THB: 0%
  • 150,001–300,000 THB: 5%
  • 300,001–500,000 THB: 10%
  • 500,001–750,000 THB: 15%
  • 750,001–1,000,000 THB: 20%
  • 1,000,001–2,000,000 THB: 25%
  • 2,000,001–5,000,000 THB: 30%
  • Over 5,000,000 THB: 35%

The Smart Strategy: Stay Under 180 Days

The simplest way to avoid Thai tax obligations: don't become a tax resident. Split your time between Thailand and another country (Bali, Malaysia, Vietnam, etc.).

Many nomads do the "visa run" naturally — 90 days in Thailand, 90 days elsewhere. This keeps you under 180 days automatically.

What's Changed in Enforcement

Thailand's Revenue Department has shifted from passive to active enforcement:

  • Immigration records are now cross-referenced with tax filings
  • The TDAC system flags anomalies between visa type and financial activity
  • DTV holders who receive payments from Thai entities face higher scrutiny
  • Data matching between banks and the Revenue Department is live

The "nobody enforces it" era is over.

Action Items

  1. Check your days: Count how many days you spent in Thailand in 2025
  2. If 180+: Get a Tax ID and file before April 8
  3. Gather docs: Bank statements, income records, transfer records
  4. Consider a tax advisor: Especially if you have income from multiple countries
  5. Plan for 2026: Structure your travel to optimize your tax situation
