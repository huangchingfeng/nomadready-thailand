# Chapter 9: Safety & Practical Tips

> **Thailand is generally very safe for foreigners.** Violent crime against tourists is rare. Your biggest risks are motorbike accidents, scams, and sunburn — not muggings.

---

## Common Scams (And How to Avoid Them)

### Tuk-Tuk Scams 🛺
**How it works:** A friendly tuk-tuk driver offers a cheap ride, then "suggests" stopping at a gem shop, suit shop, or travel agency. They earn commissions; you get pressured to buy overpriced goods.
**Solution:** Decline all "stops along the way." Use Grab instead.

### Jet Ski Damage Scams 🏍️
**How it works:** You rent a jet ski in Phuket or Koh Samui. When you return it, the owner "discovers" pre-existing scratches and demands $500-2,000 for repairs. Sometimes police side with the owner.
**Solution:** Take photos/video BEFORE renting. Or just don't rent jet skis.

### Taxi Meter Refusal 🚕
**How it works:** Bangkok taxi driver says "meter broken" and quotes a flat rate 3-5x the normal fare.
**Solution:** Close the door and walk away. Flag another taxi, or use Grab. There's always another taxi.

### Bar Bill Padding 🍺
**How it works:** In tourist nightlife areas, your bill magically includes drinks you didn't order.
**Solution:** Pay as you go. Ask for prices before ordering. Keep your own count.

### Rental Deposit Scams 🏠
**How it works:** Landlord finds "damage" at move-out and keeps your deposit.
**Solution:** Take dated photos of everything at move-in. Get the condition noted in writing.

### "Friendly Local" Temple Scams 🏛️
**How it works:** Someone near a tourist temple tells you "it's closed today" and offers to take you somewhere else (usually a gem shop).
**Solution:** Temples are almost never closed. Walk in and check yourself.

### ATM Skimming 💳
**Solution:** Use ATMs inside banks or shopping malls. Cover the keypad. Check for loose card readers.

## Laws You MUST Know

### Lèse-Majesté Law 👑
**Criticizing the Thai monarchy is a criminal offense.** Punishable by **3-15 years in prison per offense.** This applies to spoken words, social media posts, shared articles — everything. This is actively enforced.

**Rules:**
- Never make negative comments about the King, Queen, or Royal Family
- Don't share or like social media posts criticizing the monarchy
- Stand when the Royal Anthem plays (before movies, at public events)
- Don't step on Thai currency (it has the King's image)
- This is **not optional** and **not a joke** — foreigners have been arrested

### Drug Laws 💊
**Thailand has extremely strict drug laws.** Possession of hard drugs can carry a **death penalty.**

> ⚠️ **CANNABIS WARNING (Updated June 2025):** Cannabis has been **re-criminalized in Thailand** as of June 25, 2025. Recreational use, sale, and possession without a valid medical prescription are **ILLEGAL**. Penalties include fines up to **25,000 THB** and up to **3 months imprisonment**. Do not buy, carry, or use cannabis products unless you have a legitimate Thai medical prescription. The brief 2022-2025 decriminalization period is over — treat cannabis the same as any other controlled substance.

- Methamphetamine, heroin, cocaine, ecstasy: serious prison time
- Don't accept packages from strangers
- Don't carry anything through customs for someone you don't know well

### Overstay Penalties
- 500 THB/day fine (max 20,000 THB)
- **Ban tiers (voluntary departure):** 90 days–1 year: 1-year ban | 1–3 years: 3-year ban | 3–5 years: 5-year ban | 5+ years: 10-year ban
- Arrested (not voluntary): bans are doubled
- See [Chapter 1: Visa Guide](/guide/01-visa-guide) for full details

## Cultural Etiquette

### Must-Know Rules

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Wai (press palms together) when greeting | Touch anyone's head (it's sacred) |
| Remove shoes before entering homes/temples | Point your feet at people or Buddha images |

## Motorbike Safety

Motorbikes are the #1 cause of injury for foreigners in Thailand.

**Rules:**
- **Always wear a helmet** — police fine 400 THB, but the real risk is your life
- Get an **International Driving Permit** before you arrive (your home country's auto association)
- Thai traffic drives on the **left** side
- Inspect the bike before renting. Test brakes. Check tires.
- **Don't drive after drinking** — checkpoints are common
- Consider motorbike insurance (100-300 THB/month)
- Rain + Thai roads = extremely slippery

## General Safety Tips

- **Valuables:** Use hotel/condo safes. Don't flash expensive jewelry.
- **Swimming:** Watch for riptides on west coast beaches. Red flags mean don't swim.
- **Stray dogs:** Avoid touching them. Rabies exists. If bitten, go to hospital immediately.
- **Water:** Never drink tap water. Even for brushing teeth, many nomads use bottled water.

## Solo Female Travelers

**Overall: Thailand is generally safe for solo women.** Millions of women travel solo here every year. Violent crime against female tourists is rare. That said, smart precautions go a long way.

### Safety by City

| City | Safety Rating | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Chiang Mai** | Safest | Small-town feel, strong nomad community, walkable at night |
| **Bangkok** | Safe with precautions | Stick to well-lit areas, use Grab at night |
| **Phuket** | Safe in tourist areas | Avoid Patong late at night, Rawai/Kata are very safe |
| **Koh Phangan** | Safe | Watch yourself on Full Moon Party nights — it gets chaotic |

### Safe Neighborhoods (see [Chapter 3: Best Neighborhoods](/guide/03-best-neighborhoods) for details)

- **Bangkok:** Ari, Ekkamai, Thonglor, Sathorn — well-lit, populated, easy BTS access
- **Chiang Mai:** Nimman, Santitham, Old City — walkable, cafe-lined streets
- **Phuket:** Rawai, Kata, Chalong — quieter, community-oriented

### Night Safety Tips

- **Use Grab exclusively at night.** Don't hail random taxis or accept rides from strangers.
- **Avoid walking alone in quiet sois (side streets) after midnight.** Main roads are fine.
- **Avoid Khao San Road area (Bangkok) and Patong (Phuket) late at night** — drunk crowds, aggressive touts, pickpockets.
- **Share your location** with a trusted friend via Google Maps or LINE when going out.
- **Trust your gut.** If a situation feels off, leave. Thai culture makes it easy to excuse yourself politely.

### Women's Health

- **Gynecological care:** Available at international hospitals like Bumrungrad and Samitivej. Consultation: $30-60 USD. English-speaking doctors, no referral needed — just walk in.
- **Oral contraceptives:** Available OTC (over the counter) at pharmacies. 30-200 THB depending on brand. No prescription required.
- **Menstrual products:** Widely available at every 7-Eleven and Watsons. International brands (Whisper, Kotex, Laurier) stocked everywhere. Menstrual cups available at Watsons and Boots.

### Choosing Safe Accommodation

Whether you're a solo female traveler or just someone who wants peace of mind, your accommodation choice is your first line of defense. Here's what to look for.

**Must-haves for safe accommodation:**
- **24-hour security guard** at the building entrance — this is standard for Thai condos and serviced apartments
- **Key card access** for the building, elevator, and your floor. Buildings where anyone can walk in are a red flag
- **CCTV in common areas** — lobby, parking garage, hallways, pool area
- **Well-lit parking and corridors** — check this during an evening visit before signing a lease

**What to prefer:**
- **Serviced apartments** offer more security than standalone house rentals — front desk staff, key card systems, visitor registration
- **Upper floors** are generally safer than ground floor units with street-level access
- Condos in **populated areas** near BTS/MRT stations (Bangkok) or in Nimman/Old City (Chiang Mai) — busy neighborhoods are safer neighborhoods

**How to research safety:**
- Check **Google Maps reviews** for your building — search for safety-related comments (look for keywords like "security," "safe," "guard," "noisy")
- **Facebook group tip:** Post in "Bangkok Expats," "Chiang Mai Digital Nomads," or "Phuket Expats" and ask: "Is [building/area] safe for a solo female?" You'll get honest responses within hours
- Ask the building management directly: "Is there 24-hour security? Are there CCTV cameras? Is key card access required for the elevator?"

**Red flags — avoid these:**
- No security guard at any time of day
- No CCTV anywhere in the building
- Isolated location far from main roads or public transport
- Ground floor units with direct street access and no gate
- Landlord who can't answer basic security questions
- No visitor registration at the front desk

**Emergency resources:**
- **Tourist Police:** 1155 (English-speaking, 24/7) — your first call for any safety concern
- **LINE app:** Search for "Tourist Police" official LINE account for non-emergency reporting and assistance

### Cultural Notes

- **Dress modestly at temples** — shoulders and knees must be covered. Many temples provide sarongs at the entrance, but bringing your own is easier.
- **Thai culture is generally respectful toward women.** Catcalling is uncommon compared to many Western countries. Thai men are typically non-confrontational.
- Politely but firmly declining unwanted attention works. A smile and "mai ow ka" (I don't want it) handles most situations.
