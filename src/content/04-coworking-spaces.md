# Chapter 4: Top Coworking Spaces

> **You don't need a coworking space to work in Thailand** — 59% of nomads work from their apartment. But if you want fast WiFi, social connections, and a productive environment, here are the best options.

---

### Our Top Picks

**Best Free Option: AIS Design Centre** (EmQuartier, 5th Floor)
- Free entry with cafe purchase. 100-200 Mbps WiFi. Daily 10:00-22:00.
- Perfect for trying out Bangkok work life before committing to a membership.

**Best Value: True Digital Park** (Punnawithi, BTS direct)
- 321 THB day pass, 4,500 THB/month. 200-500 Mbps on True's fiber backbone.
- Thailand's largest tech hub. Herman Miller chairs. 1,200+ startups on campus.
- Direct BTS skybridge — zero rain exposure.

**Best Community: The Hive Thonglor** (Sukhumvit 55)
- 7 floors of workspace. Iconic rooftop terrace. Regular networking events + yoga.
- The place to meet other nomads. On-site spa with 50% member discount.

**Best 24/7 Access: WeWork** (Multiple locations)
- Global pass works worldwide. 24/7 for all members. Professional video call backdrops.
- Best for remote employees who need corporate credibility.

**Best Premium: The Great Room** (Gaysorn Tower)
- Ultra-premium, hotel-lobby elegance. 360° Bangkok skyline views.
- Barista coffee included. Direct BTS Chidlom connection.
- Worth it for client-facing professionals and consultants.

### Quick Comparison

| Space | Day Pass | Monthly | WiFi | Best Feature |
|-------|----------|---------|------|-------------|

| Punspace | 290 THB ($8) | 3,900 THB ($111) | 80-150 Mbps | Reliability king |
| Hub53 | 250 THB ($7) | 3,500 THB ($100) | 80-120 Mbps | Coliving combo |
| Yellow | 350 THB ($10) | 4,000 THB ($115) | 150-300 Mbps | Best internet + tech |

### Our Top Picks

**Best for Night Owls: CAMP** (Maya Mall, top floor)
- Open daily until **midnight**. Perfect for Western time zones.
- 150 THB day pass. The "living room" of Chiang Mai's nomad community.
- Gets crowded 14:00-20:00. Go early or late.

**Best for Focus: Punspace** (Nimman + Old City)
- The gold standard. Quiet, no-nonsense, productivity-focused.
- 24/7 access for monthly members. Two locations.
- Attracts experienced nomads who tried everything and settled here.

**Best for Developers: Yellow** (Near Nimman)
- 150-300 Mbps — fastest reliable internet in Chiang Mai.
- Soundproof call booths. YouTube streaming rooms. Web3/tech community.
- Regular hackathons and tech talks.

**Best for Solo Nomads: Hub53** (Near Maya)
- Coworking + coliving in one place. Community dinners. Genuine friendships.
- Perfect if you just arrived and want instant social connections.

## Pattaya

Pattaya's coworking scene is smaller but growing. Prices are the lowest of any beach city.

### Quick Comparison

| Space | Day Pass | Monthly | WiFi | Best Feature |
|-------|----------|---------|------|-------------|
| **Vwork Space** | 250 THB ($7) | 3,500 THB ($100) | 80-150 Mbps | Sea view, Jomtien |
| **Hub Pattaya** | 200 THB ($6) | 2,500 THB ($71) | 50-100 Mbps | Cheapest, central |
| **Tree Town** | 300 THB ($9) | 4,000 THB ($115) | 100-200 Mbps | Modern, community events |

### Our Top Picks

**Best View: Vwork Space** (Jomtien)
- Sea-view workspace right on Jomtien Beach. 80-150 Mbps WiFi.
- 3,500 THB/month is cheaper than most Bangkok options with an ocean view.
- Great for nomads who want beach + productivity without Phuket prices.

**Best Budget: Hub Pattaya** (Central)
- At 2,500 THB/month, this is one of the cheapest coworking memberships in Thailand.
- Central location, basic but functional. 50-100 Mbps gets the job done.
- No frills — just a desk, WiFi, and AC.

**Best Community: Tree Town** (Pattaya)
- Newest and most modern of the three. Regular community events and workshops.
- 100-200 Mbps WiFi. Good mix of local entrepreneurs and international nomads.
- The closest thing to a Chiang Mai-style coworking vibe in Pattaya.
