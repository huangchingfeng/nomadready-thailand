# Chapter 6: Banking & Money

> **Indonesia runs on cash, QRIS, and GoPay.** Your foreign credit card works at hotels and upscale restaurants, but everywhere else — from your warung lunch to your villa rent — you'll need local payment methods.

---

## Currency Basics

- **Currency:** Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)
- **Exchange rate:** ~15,500-16,000 IDR = $1 USD (March 2026)
- **Mental math shortcut:** Drop 4 zeros, then divide by 1.5. So 150,000 IDR ≈ $10.
- **Denominations:** Bills from 1,000 to 100,000 IDR. Coins exist but are rarely used.

---

## Payment Methods in Bali

| Method | Where It Works | Recommended? |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| **Cash (IDR)** | Everywhere | ✅ Always carry some |
| **QRIS (QR code)** | Most shops, cafes, restaurants | ✅ Most convenient |
| **GoPay / OVO** | Grab, Gojek, many shops | ✅ Essential |
| **Debit card (local)** | ATMs, some shops | ✅ If you open local account |
| **Foreign credit card** | Hotels, upscale restaurants, malls | 🟡 Limited + fees |
| **Wise/Revolut card** | ATMs, some shops | ✅ Best for ATM withdrawals |

### QRIS — Indonesia's QR Payment System

QRIS is everywhere. Think of it as Indonesia's version of a universal QR payment standard. Any QRIS code works with any Indonesian e-wallet (GoPay, OVO, DANA, ShopeePay) or bank app.

**To use QRIS:**
1. Open GoPay (inside Gojek app) or your Indonesian bank app
2. Scan the QR code
3. Confirm amount → done

Even street food vendors and tiny warungs accept QRIS. It's often easier than cash.

## Opening a Bank Account

### Tourist Account (Rekening Turis) — No KITAS Required

Most major banks now offer accounts for tourists using just a passport.

**Requirements:**
- Valid passport
- Indonesian phone number (your Telkomsel SIM works)
- Initial deposit: ~$2,000 USD equivalent
- In-person visit to a bank branch

**Limitations:**
- Balance cap: typically $50,000 USD
- Some features restricted (no credit cards, limited online transfers)
- May require higher minimum balance than local accounts

**Best banks for tourists:**
- **BCA** — Largest ATM network, best app, most widely accepted
- **Mandiri** — State-owned, extensive coverage, good for transfers
- **BNI** — Good English support at main branches

### KITAS Account — Full Access

If you have an E33G KITAS, you can open a full-service account with all features.

**Additional requirements:**
- e-KITAS document
- NPWP (tax ID) — recommended but not always required
- Domicile letter from local village head (surat keterangan domisili) or formal lease
- Sponsor/employer letter (for work KITAS)

### Why Bother with a Local Account?

- Pay rent without international transfer fees
- Use QRIS and GoPay natively
- Receive local transfers (split bills, freelance payments)
- Lower ATM withdrawal fees
- Some landlords only accept local bank transfer

## Wise, Revolut & Multi-Currency Cards

If you don't open a local account, a **Wise** or **Revolut** card is your best friend.

### Wise (Recommended)
- Mid-market exchange rate with transparent fees (typically 0.4-0.8%)
- ATM withdrawals: free up to $100/month, then 1.75% fee
- **Top up GoPay and OVO** directly from Wise — this is the killer feature
- Hold IDR balance in your Wise account
- Contactless payments work at most terminals

### Revolut
- Similar to Wise, slightly different fee structure
- Free ATM withdrawals up to $200/month (standard plan)
- Good app, supports IDR

### Pro Tips
- **Always choose IDR** when an ATM or terminal asks "charge in your home currency?" — declining Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC) saves you 3-8%
- **Wise → GoPay** is the cheapest way to get money into Indonesia's payment ecosystem without a local bank account

## ATM Survival Guide

### Fees
- Indonesian bank fee: **30,000-75,000 IDR ($2-5)** per withdrawal
- Your home bank fee: varies (Wise/Revolut minimize this)
- Total: typically $3-7 per withdrawal

### Withdrawal Limits
- Most ATMs: **1,250,000-3,000,000 IDR ($80-200)** per transaction
- BCA gold machines: up to **10,000,000 IDR ($650)** — look for these
- Daily limit depends on your home bank

### Which ATMs to Use
- ✅ **BCA** — Largest network, highest limits, most reliable
- ✅ **Mandiri** — Good availability, reliable
- ✅ **BNI** — Widely available
- ⚠️ **Standalone ATMs** (not attached to a bank) — higher skimming risk, avoid if possible

### Safety
- Use ATMs **inside bank branches** or **inside malls** — never standalone roadside machines
- Cover the keypad when entering your PIN
- Check for loose card slot fittings (skimming devices)
- If an ATM eats your card, go directly to the bank branch (during hours) or call your bank immediately

## Money Exchange

### Where to Exchange
- ✅ **Authorized money changers** — Look for official license displayed. Central Kuta, PT Central Kuta is reputable.
- ✅ **Bank counters** — Slightly worse rates but zero risk
- ⚠️ **Street money changers** — Scams are common (short-counting, hidden fees, sleight of hand). Avoid.
- ❌ **Airport exchange** — Worst rates. Only exchange minimum on arrival.

### Tips
- Bring clean, unmarked USD or EUR bills. Torn, written-on, or old-series bills may be rejected.
- $100 bills get better rates than smaller denominations
- Always count your money at the counter before leaving

## Tipping Culture

Tipping is not mandatory in Bali but is appreciated:

- **Warungs/local restaurants:** Not expected. Rounding up is nice.
- **Cafes/mid-range restaurants:** 10,000-20,000 IDR ($0.65-1.30) if service was good
- **Upscale restaurants:** Often include 10-15% service charge + 11% tax. Check the bill.
- **Spa/massage:** 20,000-50,000 IDR ($1.30-3.25) is generous
- **Drivers (day hire):** 50,000-100,000 IDR ($3.25-6.50)
- **Hotel staff:** 20,000-50,000 IDR for porters, housekeeping
