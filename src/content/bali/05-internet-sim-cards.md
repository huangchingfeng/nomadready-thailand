# Chapter 5: Internet & SIM Cards

> **Bali's internet has improved dramatically, but it's not Thailand.** In Canggu coworking spaces, you'll get 100-270 Mbps. In a random villa in Uluwatu, you might struggle for 10 Mbps. Location and preparation matter here.

**Where to buy:**
- **Airport:** Ngurah Rai arrivals hall — Telkomsel and XL booths right after customs. Slightly marked up but convenient.
- **Phone shops:** Everywhere in Canggu, Seminyak, Ubud. Better prices than airport.
- **Online:** Order through Telkomsel/XL apps after getting a local number.

**Bring your passport** — SIM registration is mandatory in Indonesia.

> 💡 **5G status:** Available in parts of Canggu, Seminyak, Denpasar, and Nusa Dua. Don't count on it — 4G/LTE (15-50 Mbps on mobile) is the reliable standard across Bali.

## Home Internet (For Stays of 1+ Month)

### Providers

| Provider | Speed | Monthly | Availability |
|----------|-------|---------|-------------|
| **IndiHome (Telkom)** | 30-300 Mbps | 300K-700K IDR ($20-45) | Widest coverage |
| **Biznet** | 75-500 Mbps | 350K-900K IDR ($23-58) | Best in urban areas |
| **ICONNET** | 50-300 Mbps | 200K-500K IDR ($13-32) | Growing, competitive |
| **BaliFiber** | 30-200 Mbps | 300K-600K IDR ($20-39) | Bali-specific |

### The Reality Check
- **Coverage varies wildly** by location. Your villa in central Canggu might have fiber; a villa 2 km away might not.
- **Ask your landlord** what's already installed before signing a lease. Pre-existing fiber is a major advantage.
- **Installation takes 1-3 weeks** — not ideal for short stays.
- **Alternative: Telkomsel Orbit** — a 4G/5G mobile modem. No installation, works anywhere with cellular coverage. ~300K-500K IDR/month. Great backup or primary if fiber isn't available.

### Installation for Foreigners
- Usually arranged through your landlord (they need to be present or authorize)
- Some providers accept foreigner applications with passport + KITAS
- Short-stay solution: negotiate internet inclusion in your villa rent

## Pro Tips

1. **Always have a backup.** Your villa WiFi will go down. Have mobile data ready.
2. **Test before committing.** Run a speed test at potential villas and coworking spaces. ookla.com/speedtest works.
3. **Coworking ≠ cafes for internet.** Coworking spaces invest in business-grade connections with redundancy. Cafes don't.
4. **Video calls:** For reliable Zoom/Meet calls, you need 15+ Mbps upload. Confirm this specifically.
5. **Rainy season impact:** Power outages are more common Nov-Mar. A laptop with good battery + mobile hotspot = your lifeline.
6. **Power banks:** Carry one. Power infrastructure is less reliable than Thailand's.
