# Chapter 11: Your First 30 Days in Bali

> **The difference between a great Bali experience and a frustrating one is preparation.** This checklist walks you through everything — from before you board your flight to being fully set up as a productive digital nomad.

---

## Before You Leave Home

### Documents & Admin
- [ ] Passport valid 18+ months (required for E33G; 6+ months for other visas)
- [ ] International Driving Permit (IDP) — **get this before you leave.** You cannot get one in Bali. Required for legal motorbike riding and insurance claims.
- [ ] Travel insurance active ([SafetyWing](https://safetywing.com/?referenceID=26492570&utm_source=26492570&utm_medium=Ambassador), Genki, or your choice — see [Chapter 7](/guide/07-healthcare))
- [ ] Scan all documents (passport, IDP, insurance, bank cards) → store in cloud + email to yourself
- [ ] E33G applicants: start the visa process 4-6 weeks before travel
- [ ] Notify your bank you're traveling to Indonesia (avoid card blocks)

### Tech Setup
- [ ] Download **eSIM** (Airalo or Holafly) — activate on landing for instant connectivity
- [ ] Download apps: **Grab**, **Gojek** (ride-hailing), **Google Maps** (offline Bali map), **Wise** (money transfers), **XE** (currency converter)
- [ ] VPN installed — [NordVPN](https://go.nordvpn.net/aff_c?offer_id=15&aff_id=143233&url_id=902) recommended (some sites/services are blocked in Indonesia)
- [ ] Backup laptop charger + universal adapter (Indonesia uses Type C/F plugs, 230V)
- [ ] Power bank (charged) — Bali has more power outages than you're used to

### Money
- [ ] [Wise](https://wise.com/invite/dic/chingfengh7) or Revolut card loaded with funds
- [ ] Some USD or EUR cash ($200-300) for emergency exchange on arrival
- [ ] Confirm your debit/credit card works internationally and check foreign ATM fee policies

## Day 1: Arrival

### At Ngurah Rai Airport (DPS)
- [ ] Clear immigration (have return ticket and accommodation proof ready)
- [ ] Buy **Telkomsel SIM card** at arrivals hall booth (~150K IDR for 25GB/30 days)
- [ ] Exchange small amount of cash if needed (airport rates are acceptable for small amounts)
- [ ] Grab/Gojek to your accommodation (or pre-arranged airport pickup — typically 200-350K IDR to Canggu)

> 💡 **Airport exit tip:** The airport has an official taxi counter and a Grab/Gojek pickup zone. Use the pickup zone — it's cheaper. Walk past the aggressive taxi touts.

### First Night
- [ ] Test your villa/hotel WiFi — run a speed test (speedtest.net)
- [ ] Confirm your eSIM + physical SIM both work
- [ ] Get some rest. Jet lag is real. Don't make any decisions today.

## Days 2-3: Orientation

### Getting Around
- [ ] Rent a motorbike — ask your accommodation or nearby rental shops. 700K-1.2M IDR/month for automatic scooter.
- [ ] **Helmet check:** Make sure it's a proper helmet (not a decorative coconut shell). Your insurance requires it.
- [ ] Practice riding in a quiet area first if you're not experienced. Bali traffic is no joke.
- [ ] If not comfortable on a bike, Grab/Gojek everywhere is viable but adds up ($5-15/day)

### Exploring Your Area
- [ ] Walk/ride around your neighborhood. Find: nearest grocery shop, pharmacy, laundry, ATM, warung (local restaurant)
- [ ] Try 2-3 local warungs — find your go-to cheap meal spot
- [ ] Locate the nearest coworking space — most offer free trials or day passes
- [ ] Check the nearest hospital/clinic location (save address in Google Maps)

### Money Setup
- [ ] Withdraw IDR from an ATM (use bank-attached ATMs: BCA, Mandiri, BNI)
  - Choose IDR, **decline** dynamic currency conversion
  - Typical limit: 1.25-3M IDR per transaction ($80-200)
- [ ] Download **GoPay** (in Gojek app) or **OVO** — Indonesia's mobile payment apps
  - Top up via Wise, convenience store, or ATM
  - QRIS (QR code payment) works almost everywhere

## Days 4-7: Setting Up Work

### Coworking
- [ ] Try 2-3 coworking spaces on day passes
- [ ] Test internet speed at each (ask for speed test, check upload speed for video calls)
- [ ] Check operating hours, noise level, and air conditioning
- [ ] If you found your spot, buy a weekly or monthly pass

### Home Office Setup (if working from villa)
- [ ] Assess your villa internet — is it good enough for daily work?
- [ ] If not: ask landlord about fiber installation or get a Telkomsel Orbit modem
- [ ] Buy a basic desk fan if your room doesn't have good AC (common in budget villas)
- [ ] Consider a laptop stand + external keyboard — your neck will thank you

### Communication
- [ ] Test a video call from your primary work location
- [ ] Set up your time zone in work tools (Bali is GMT+8, same as Singapore/Taipei)
- [ ] Inform your team/clients of your time zone and availability windows

## Week 2: Getting Settled

### Accommodation Decision
- [ ] If your first place isn't right, now is the time to look for long-term housing
- [ ] Check Facebook groups for rentals: "Bali Long Term Rentals," "[Your Area] Community"
- [ ] Walk around your preferred neighborhood looking for "For Rent" signs (best deals are offline)
- [ ] Negotiate: 3+ month stays should get 10-20% off monthly rate. Always negotiate.

### Health & Wellness
- [ ] Register with a nearby gym or yoga studio if that's your thing
  - Gym: 500K-2M IDR/month ($32-130)
  - Yoga: 100K-200K IDR per class ($6.50-13), or monthly unlimited 1-2M IDR ($65-130)
- [ ] Stock up on essentials: sunscreen (expensive in Bali — bring from home), mosquito repellent, basic first aid
- [ ] Find a pharmacy — many prescription medications are available over the counter

### Banking (If Staying 2+ Months)
- [ ] Consider opening a **tourist bank account (Rekening Turis)** — BCA or Mandiri
  - Passport only, no KITAS needed
  - Balance cap $50K, minimum deposit ~$2K
  - Makes rent payments, QRIS, and local transfers much easier
- [ ] Set up Wise for receiving international payments → transfer to local account

## Week 3-4: Building Your Routine

### Social & Community
- [ ] Attend a coworking community event or meetup
- [ ] Join relevant Facebook groups and WhatsApp communities
- [ ] Try a new area for a day trip — if you're in Canggu, visit Ubud (or vice versa)
- [ ] Find your regular spots: morning coffee place, lunch warung, sunset spot

### Admin & Long-Term Planning
- [ ] If on VoA: schedule your 30-day extension at immigration (or have your visa agent handle it)
- [ ] If on B211A: note your extension dates in your calendar — don't overstay
- [ ] Track your days in Indonesia for the 183-day tax threshold
- [ ] Set up regular laundry service (most areas have laundry by the kilo: 7-10K IDR/kg = $0.50-0.65/kg)

### The Bali Lifestyle
- [ ] Learn basic Bahasa Indonesia — even "terima kasih" (thank you) and "berapa?" (how much?) go a long way
- [ ] Respect temple ceremonies — you'll encounter processions and ceremonies regularly. Stop, wait, smile.
- [ ] Try at least one "Bali thing" — sunrise hike (Mt. Batur), rice terrace walk (Tegallalang), temple visit (Uluwatu), surf lesson
