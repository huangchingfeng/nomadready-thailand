### Installation for Foreigners

**Documents needed:**
- Passport with valid visa
- Rental agreement
- Landlord's house registration book (tabien baan) — ask your landlord
- Landlord's Thai ID copy (signed)

**Process:** Visit a provider shop → sign 12-month contract → installation in 3-7 days → THB 2,000-3,000 installation fee (often waived)

**Pro tips:**
- Many apartments already have internet installed — ask to keep/upgrade the existing line
- Some condos have building-wide packages — ask management
- If your landlord puts the account in their name, the process is much simpler

## VPN

Thailand **does not block VPNs.** Unlike China or Iran, you can use any VPN service freely.

**When you need a VPN:**
- Accessing home country banking apps that geo-block Thai IPs
- Streaming services — unlock your home Netflix/Hulu/BBC iPlayer library
- Corporate VPN connections for enterprise remote workers
- Extra security on public cafe WiFi

**Top picks:**

| VPN | Monthly | Speed Impact | Servers | Best For |
|-----|---------|-------------|---------|----------|
| **NordVPN** | $3.49/mo (2-year plan) | 15-25% reduction | 60+ countries | Best overall |
| **ExpressVPN** | $6.67/mo (1-year plan) | 20-30% reduction | 94 countries | Most reliable connections |
| **Surfshark** | $2.49/mo (2-year plan) | 20-35% reduction | 100+ countries | Best budget pick, unlimited devices |

**Pro tip:** Connect to the server closest to your client's location for the best video call quality.

## Upload Speeds (Critical for Video Calls)

Most people obsess over download speed. For remote work, **upload speed and latency matter more.** Download speed doesn't matter for Zoom calls. Upload speed and latency do.

### Upload Speed by Connection Type

| Connection | Download | Upload | Video Call Quality |
|-----------|----------|--------|-------------------|
| Home fiber (AIS/True) | 500-1,000 Mbps | 100-300 Mbps | Flawless |
| 5G (urban) | 100-350 Mbps | 30-80 Mbps | Excellent |
| Cafe WiFi (premium) | 80-300 Mbps | 30-100 Mbps | Good to excellent |
| Cafe WiFi (average) | 30-80 Mbps | 10-50 Mbps | Good |
| 4G LTE hotspot | 30-80 Mbps | 10-30 Mbps | Acceptable |
| Cafe WiFi (basic) | 10-30 Mbps | 5-15 Mbps | Risky for HD video |

**Minimum requirements for video calls:**
- Zoom/Google Meet: 3.8 Mbps upload for HD video
- Group calls (gallery view): 3.0 Mbps upload
- Screen sharing: Add 1-2 Mbps upload

Most Thai connections clear these minimums easily. The risk is cheap cafe WiFi during peak hours. Test upload speed at **fast.com** (click "show more info") before your call.

## Time Zone Management (GMT+7)

Thailand is **UTC+7 year-round** — no daylight saving time changes. This is a major advantage for scheduling.

| Your Clients | Time Difference | Overlap Strategy |
|-------------|----------------|-----------------|
| US West Coast (PST) | -15 hours | Morning calls: 8am Thai = 5pm previous day PST |
| US East Coast (EST) | -12 hours | Morning calls: 8am Thai = 8pm previous day EST |
| UK (GMT) | -7 hours | Afternoon calls: 3pm Thai = 8am UK |
| Central Europe (CET) | -6 hours | Afternoon calls: 2pm Thai = 8am CET |
| Japan/Korea (JST/KST) | +2 hours | Very compatible — almost same timezone |
| Australia (AEST) | +3 hours | Morning Thai = afternoon Australia |

### South America → Thailand

The time difference from South America is the biggest adjustment for LATAM nomads. Plan your work schedule around it.

| Your Home City | Timezone | Difference to Bangkok | Example |
|---------------|----------|----------------------|---------|
| São Paulo / Buenos Aires | BRT/ART (UTC-3) | **+10 hours** | 8am Bangkok = 10pm previous day São Paulo |
| Bogotá / Lima | COT/PET (UTC-5) | **+12 hours** | 8am Bangkok = 8pm previous day Bogotá |
| Mexico City | CST (UTC-6) | **+13 hours** | 8am Bangkok = 7pm previous day CDMX |

**If your employer is in the US (common for LATAM remote workers):**
- Working for a US EST company from Thailand: you're 12 hours ahead
- Working for a US PST company from Thailand: you're 15 hours ahead
- This is actually similar to working US hours from South America — you're already used to some overlap gymnastics

**Overlap strategy for LATAM nomads:**
- **Work early mornings (6-10 AM Thai time)** to catch US afternoon/evening hours. This gives you 4 hours of real-time overlap.
- **Negotiate async work** — if your team already works async (Slack, GitHub, Linear), the timezone barely matters. Ship your work by end of your Thai day, and your US team picks it up in their morning.
- **Split your day:** Some LATAM nomads work 7-11 AM (overlap hours) + 2-6 PM (deep focus). This leaves evenings completely free for exploring Thailand.
- **The upside:** Your afternoons and evenings in Thailand are completely yours. No late-night Zoom calls like you might have had working US hours from Brazil.

**Thailand's timezone is extremely favorable for EU and Asia-Pacific clients.** Afternoon overlap with Europe is natural and comfortable. Japan/Korea are practically the same timezone.

**US clients require morning discipline.** If your clients are in New York, your 7-9am is their 6-8pm previous day. Many US-serving nomads in Thailand adopt an early morning routine: calls at 7-9am Thai time, then free afternoons.

**Pro tip:** Block your calendar in advance. Set "Thai hours" and "client hours" and protect your boundaries. The biggest burnout risk for nomads is being available 24/7 across timezones.

## Emergency Backup Plan

If your internet dies during a critical deadline:

1. **Phone hotspot** — Your Thai SIM with unlimited data (30-80 Mbps on 4G)
2. **Nearest 7-Eleven** — Every one has True WiFi (10-30 Mbps). Free with True SIM.
3. **Hotel lobby** — Walk into any 4-5 star hotel, order a lobby coffee, use their WiFi (50-100 Mbps)
4. **Shopping mall** — CentralWorld, Siam Paragon, EmQuartier all have free WiFi
5. **Same-day pocket WiFi delivery** — Providers deliver within hours in Bangkok

### Redundancy Checklist

- [ ] Thai SIM with unlimited data (AIS recommended)
- [ ] eSIM loaded as backup (activate when needed)
- [ ] 3 nearest coworking spaces bookmarked
- [ ] 3 nearest work cafes bookmarked
- [ ] Portable battery pack charged (20,000 mAh+)
- [ ] Critical documents saved offline
